Temperature records

Thu 14 Dec 2017, 11:23 AM AEDT

In 1997 Australia experienced a few heatwaves.

---

It's so hot, but it’s always been hot in summer right… Everyone remembers burning bitumen under foot, restless hot nights, the mad dash to the beach across burning sand.

Well let’s get one thing straight first, it’s getting hotter, the world is getting hotter:

But 1 degree, that’s not really that different…. Well it’s not as simple as that.

When were you born?

SECTION 2: THERE’S A HIGHER CHANCE SUMMER WILL BE HOTTER

In 1983 there was a 5 per cent chance that your first summer would be a swelterer, already up from the 20th Century average of 2 per cent, but for a child born today it’s a 11 per cent.

5 years old - your first trip the to the beach, by this point you’d experience one scorcher of a summer - 1984, but a kid born 5 years ago, all of the summers have been hotter than that summer.

[temperature of summer - over the 20th century average]


SECTION 3: THERE ARE MORE REALLY HOT DAYS EVERY YEAR

Ahh ten, running around in the sun, when you were 10 you’d experience this many extreme summers.

Kids today are just soft, right, right? Someone who is 10 has already experienced this many extreme summers.

The summer of your 10th birthday had 2 days over 35 degrees, those days where you lay under the fan. For a kid who turned 10 last year they had 22 days over 35 and that was a La Nina year.

But what about someone who is born today, well by the time they’re 10, they’ll have experienced this many extreme summers - in fact extreme summers are the new normal.

SECTION 4: YES THERE’S ALWAYS BEEN HOT DAYS BUT ACTUALLY IT’S MUCH HOTTER AND THOSE RECORDS FROM A LONG TIME AGO ARE GETTING FEW AND FEWER

What about that old guy at the pub who’s always talking about the summer of 54 when he fried a mixed grill on the bonnet of his FJ holden- well there were a few hot summers, but there weren’t that many. In fact by the time he was your age he’d only experienced 2 really hot summers. And yes Bourke did swelter through the hottest summer it’s ever experienced in 1936. But look how many other town records have broken since then….

And look how many town records have been broken since 2000.

And look how many cold records have been broken since 2000.

SECTION 5: THE SAME WEATHER FROM WHEN YOU WERE A KID WOULD NOW BE MUCH HOTTER

Well how do we know it’s climate change - well remember 1972 - you were XX - the weather conditions were exactly the same as 2013, but in 1972 it was only XX hot in Australia whereas in 2013 it was the hottest year on record.
Use the case study of 2013 vs 1961 - same conditions different temperatures.

SECTION 6: SUMMERS ARE GETTING LONGER

But What about where I live? - do we do this as a way of geolocated it at the end?

SECTION 7: FOR A KID BORN TODAY IT’S NOT JUST GOING TO BE HOTTER, IT’S GOING TO BE MORE DEADLY
